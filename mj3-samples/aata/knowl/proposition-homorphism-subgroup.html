Proposition 11.4.

Let \(\phi : G_1 \rightarrow G_2\) be a homomorphism of groups. Then

  1. If \(e\) is the identity of \(G_1\text{,}\) then \(\phi( e)\) is the identity of \(G_2\text{;}\)

  2. For any element \(g \in G_1\text{,}\) \(\phi( g^{-1}) = [\phi( g )]^{- 1}\text{;}\)

  3. If \(H_1\) is a subgroup of \(G_1\text{,}\) then \(\phi( H_1 )\) is a subgroup of \(G_2\text{;}\)

  4. If \(H_2\) is a subgroup of \(G_2\text{,}\) then \(\phi^{-1}(H_2) = \{ g \in G _1: \phi(g) \in H_2 \}\) is a subgroup of \(G_1\text{.}\) Furthermore, if \(H_2\) is normal in \(G_2\text{,}\) then \(\phi^{-1}(H_2)\) is normal in \(G_1\text{.}\)

Proof.
