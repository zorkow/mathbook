Suppose that we wish to color the vertices of a square with two different colors, say black and white. We might suspect that there would be \(2^4=16\) different colorings. However, some of these colorings are equivalent. If we color the first vertex black and the remaining vertices white, it is the same as coloring the second vertex black and the remaining ones white since we could obtain the second coloring simply by rotating the square \(90^\circ\) (FigureÂ 14.17).
