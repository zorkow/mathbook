Let \(B\) be a Boolean algebra. Then

  1. \(a \vee I = I\) and \(a \wedge O = O\) for all \(a \in B\text{.}\)

  2. If \(a \vee b = a \vee c\) and \(a \wedge b = a \wedge c\) for \(a, b, c \in B\text{,}\) then \(b = c\text{.}\)

  3. If \(a \vee b = I\) and \(a \wedge b = O\text{,}\) then \(b = a'\text{.}\)

  4. \((a')'=a\) for all \(a \in B\text{.}\)

  5. \(I' = O\) and \(O' = I\text{.}\)

  6. \((a \vee b)' = a' \wedge b'\) and \((a \wedge b)' = a' \vee b'\) (De Morgan's Laws).
