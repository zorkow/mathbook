Theorem 18.4.

Let \(D\) be an integral domain. Then \(D\) can be embedded in a field of fractions \(F_D\text{,}\) where any element in \(F_D\) can be expressed as the quotient of two elements in \(D\text{.}\) Furthermore, the field of fractions \(F_D\) is unique in the sense that if \(E\) is any field containing \(D\text{,}\) then there exists a map \(\psi : F_D \rightarrow E\) giving an isomorphism with a subfield of \(E\) such that \(\psi(a) = a\) for all elements \(a \in D\text{,}\) where we identify \(a\) with its image in \(F_D\text{.}\)
