The point here is that this result (coset \(1\) times coset \(3\) is coset \(2\)) should always be the same, no matter which elements we pick from the two cosets to form p. So let us do it again, but this time we will not simply choose the first element from each of coset \(1\) and coset \(3\text{,}\) instead we will choose the third element of coset \(1\) and the second element of coset \(3\) (remember, we are counting from zero!).
