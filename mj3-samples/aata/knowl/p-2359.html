Here is a rather interesting group, one of the \(26\) sporadic simple groups, known as the Higman-Sims group, \(HS\text{.}\) The generators used below come from the representation on 100 points in GAP format, available off of web.mat.bham.ac.uk/atlas/v2.0/spor/HS/. Two generators of just order \(2\) and order \(5\) (as you can esily see), generating exactly \(44\,352\,000\) elements, but no normal subgroups. Amazing.
