From about 1955 to 1970, Alexander Grothendieck dominated the field of algebraic geometry. Pierre Deligne, a student of Grothendieck, solved several of Weil's number-theoretic conjectures. One of the most recent contributions to algebra and number theory is Gerd Falting's proof of the Mordell-Weil conjecture. This conjecture of Mordell and Weil essentially says that certain polynomials \(p(x, y)\) in \({\mathbb Z}[x,y]\) have only a finite number of integral solutions.
