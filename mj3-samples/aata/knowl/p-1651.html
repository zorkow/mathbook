Let \(G\) be a group and \(N\) be a subgroup of \(G\text{.}\) Then the following statements are equivalent.

  1. The subgroup \(N\) is normal in \(G\text{.}\)

  2. For all \(g \in G\text{,}\) \(gNg^{-1} \subset N\text{.}\)

  3. For all \(g \in G\text{,}\) \(gNg^{-1} = N\text{.}\)
