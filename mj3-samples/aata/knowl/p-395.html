The integers mod \(n\) form a group under addition modulo \(n\text{.}\) Consider \({\mathbb Z}_5\text{,}\) consisting of the equivalence classes of the integers \(0\text{,}\) \(1\text{,}\) \(2\text{,}\) \(3\text{,}\) and \(4\text{.}\) We define the group operation on \({\mathbb Z}_5\) by modular addition. We write the binary operation on the group additively; that is, we write \(m + n\text{.}\) The element 0 is the identity of the group and each element in \({\mathbb Z}_5\) has an inverse. For instance, \(2 + 3 = 3 + 2 = 0\text{.}\) Figure 3.10 is a Cayley table for \({\mathbb Z}_5\text{.}\) By Proposition 3.4, \({\mathbb Z}_n = \{0, 1, \ldots, n-1 \}\) is a group under the binary operation of addition mod \(n\text{.}\)
