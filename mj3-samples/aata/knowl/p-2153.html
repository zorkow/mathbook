Two switching functions \(f\) and \(g\) are equivalent if \(g\) can be obtained from \(f\) by a permutation of the input variables. For example, \(g(a, b, c) = f(b, c, a)\text{.}\) In this case \(g \sim f\) via the permutation \((acb)\text{.}\) In the case of switching functions of two variables, the permutation \((ab)\) reduces 16 possible switching functions to 12 equivalent functions since

\begin{align*} f_2 & \sim f_4\\ f_3 & \sim f_5\\ f_{10} & \sim f_{12}\\ f_{11} & \sim f_{13}\text{.} \end{align*}
