Conversely, suppose that \(p(x)\) is irreducible over \(F[x]\text{.}\) Let \(I\) be an ideal in \(F[x]\) containing \(\langle p(x) \rangle\text{.}\) By TheoremÂ 17.20, \(I\) is a principal ideal; hence, \(I = \langle f(x) \rangle\) for some \(f(x) \in F[x]\text{.}\) Since \(p(x) \in I\text{,}\) it must be the case that \(p(x) = f(x) g(x)\) for some \(g(x) \in F[x]\text{.}\) However, \(p(x)\) is irreducible; hence, either \(f(x)\) or \(g(x)\) is a constant polynomial. If \(f(x)\) is constant, then \(I = F[x]\) and we are done. If \(g(x)\) is constant, then \(f(x)\) is a constant multiple of \(I\) and \(I = \langle p(x) \rangle\text{.}\) Thus, there are no proper ideals of \(F[x]\) that properly contain \(\langle p(x)\rangle\text{.}\)
