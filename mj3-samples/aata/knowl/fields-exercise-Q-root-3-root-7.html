Exercise 22.

Show that \({\mathbb Q}( \sqrt{3}, \sqrt{7}\, ) = {\mathbb Q}( \sqrt{3} + \sqrt{7}\, )\text{.}\) Extend your proof to show that \({\mathbb Q}( \sqrt{a}, \sqrt{b}\, ) = {\mathbb Q}( \sqrt{a} + \sqrt{b}\, )\text{,}\) where \(a \neq b\) and neither \(a\) nor \(b\) is a perfect square.
