Example 8.8.

Suppose that \(p = 0.995\) and a \(500\)-bit message is sent. The probability that the message was sent error-free is

\begin{equation*} p^{n} = (0.995)^{500} \approx 0.082\text{.} \end{equation*}

The probability of exactly one error occurring is

\begin{equation*} \binom{n}{1} qp^{n - 1}= 500(0.005)(0.995)^{499} \approx 0.204\text{.} \end{equation*}

The probability of exactly two errors is

\begin{equation*} \binom{n}{2} q^{2}p^{n - 2}= \frac{500 \cdot 499}{2}(0.005)^{2}(0.995)^{498} \approx 0.257\text{.} \end{equation*}

The probability of more than two errors is approximately

\begin{equation*} 1 - 0.082 - 0.204 - 0.257 = 0.457\text{.} \end{equation*}
